About

The Knizhnik–Zamolodchikov equations are differential operators arising naturally in conformal field theory and the representation theory of quantum groups. These notes explore the KZ equations, their many generalisations—including quantum, dynamical, and elliptic variants—and their deep connections to various areas of mathematics and physics, from topological field theory and factorisation algebras to cohomological Hall algebras and vertex operator algebras.

Questions

  • Is the data of dynamical KZ equivalent to extending factorisation functors to Stokes exit paths, as defined by Porta–Teyssier?
  • What is the analogous factorisation structure for representations of the quantum affine algebra Uq(ĝ)?
  • Does Rep Uq(ĝ) carry a crystal structure when factorised over Ran(ℂ/ℤ)? Is the monodromy around the diagonal equal to R0(z)?
  • What is the precise relation between abelian qKZ and full qKZ? Does it relate to the Gauss decomposition R(z) = R−(z)R0(z)R+(z) of the R-matrix?
  • What is the vertex algebraic analogue of the extra Stokes structure and dynamical KZ equations for Rep Uq(g)?
  • What are the KZ equations for boundaries coupling a quasitriangular Hopf algebra H with a vertex algebra V, following recent work of Brochier–Caviglia–Dimofte–Nikshych?
  • What is the correct replacement of the moduli stack and cohomology theory to produce dynamical (q)KZ equations? Kapranov–Schechtman suggest rapid decay cohomology.
  • Are there q-difference equations attached to coherent sheaves on algebraic surfaces Coh0(S), quivers with potential (Q,W), and more generally to any doubled cohomological Hall algebra D(A)?
